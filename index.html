02 — STRONG NUCLEAR FORCE

The Glue of the Nucleus

Protons carry positive charge and should repel one another. Yet nuclei hold together — bound by the strongest force in nature, acting only at extremely short range.

Relative Strength1 (strongest)
Range~10-15 m
Force CarrierGluon
ApplicationNuclear Energy

03 — ELECTROMAGNETIC FORCE

Fields, Charge & Light

Acting on any electric charge across infinite range, this force governs atoms, chemistry, electricity, magnetism, and every photon of light that reaches your eye.

Relative Strength10-2
RangeInfinite
Force CarrierPhoton
ApplicationElectronics, Light

Opposite charges attract. Like charges repel.

Beta decay: a neutron transforms into a proton.

04 — WEAK NUCLEAR FORCE

The Force of Transformation

The weak force doesn't bind — it transforms. It lets one type of particle decay into another, powering radioactivity and the nuclear fusion inside the Sun.

Relative Strength10-6
Range~10-18 m
Force CarrierW / Z Bosons
ApplicationRadioactivity, the Sun

05 — GRAVITATIONAL FORCE

The Weakest, Widest Reach

Gravity is by far the weakest fundamental force, yet its infinite range and constant attraction shape planets, stars, and the large-scale structure of the cosmos.

Relative Strength10-38 (weakest)
RangeInfinite
Force CarrierGraviton (theoretical)
ApplicationPlanetary Motion

An apple falls. The Moon orbits Earth. Both obey the same law.
